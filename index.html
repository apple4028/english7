單字 詞性 音節 KK音標 中文意思 例句
theory
n. [C, U] the-o-ry [`θiərɪ] 學說；理論

Darwin’s theory of evolution is one of the most important sets of ideas in the history of science.

psychology
n. [U] psy-chol-o-gy [saɪ`kɑlədʒɪ] 心理學

Wes Craven majored in psychology, which taught him a lot about the human mind and thus helped him become a successful horror film director.

demonstrate
vt. dem-on-strate [`dɛmənˏstret] 表達

Luke has demonstrated much excitement and interest in his new job.

interpret
vt. in-ter-pret [ɪn`tɝprɪt] 理解；詮釋

One can often interpret what a person is saying in a different language simply by observing his or her body language.

stiff
adj. stiff [stɪf] 僵硬的

After a long, hard day in the mines, the miners’ arms were stiff and sore from all the digging.

scratch
vt. scratch [skrætʃ] 搔抓

According to the article, it’s better not to scratch an itchy mosquito bite because that just makes it worse.

exhibit
vt. ex-hib-it [ɪg`zɪbɪt] 表現；表露

The bull exhibited its dislike for the stranger by angrily scratching the ground and pointing its large, sharp horns at her.

gesture
n. [C] ges-ture [`dʒɛstʃɚ] 手勢；姿勢

The traffic officer made a gesture at the driver to show that she could now safely turn right.

moreover
adv. more-o-ver [mor`ovɚ] 此外

The Danube is the second longest river in Europe. Moreover, it’s the river that flows through the most countries in the world.

conscious
adj. con-scious [`kɑnʃəs] 意識到；注意到

When Jim woke up after the accident, he slowly became conscious of the machine beeping next to his hospital bed.

observe
vt. ob-serve [əb`zɝv] 觀察

The class closely observed their yoga teacher’s graceful movements so that they could copy her.

reverse
n. sing. re-verse [rɪ`vɝs] 相反的情況或事物

Some say Leila is a liar, but quite the reverse is true. She is one of the most honest people I have ever known.

significant
adj. sig-nif-i-cant [sɪg`nɪfəkənt] 重要的；顯著的

The researchers couldn’t find any significant evidence to support the theory.

circumstance
n. [C] cir-cum-stance [`sɝkəmˏstæns] 條件；狀況

Under no circumstances can visitors to the museum touch or remove any artworks from the exhibition.

initially
adv. in-i-tial-ly [ɪ`nɪʃəlɪ] 起初

Initially, the bruises on Arthur’s leg were deep purple. Then, as they healed, they slowly turned brown.

glance
vi. glance [glæns] 匆匆一瞥

Although Arnold didn’t want his date to know he was bored, he still glanced at the clock now and then.

automatically
adv. au-to-mat-i-cal-ly [ˏɔtə`mætɪkḷɪ] 無意識地；不假思索地

Close observation has shown that we automatically raise our eyebrows more when we speak to people we are attracted to.

track
vt. track [træk] 追蹤

With this app, one can track friends’ movements and spot their locations at any time.

adore
vt. a-dore [ ə`dɔr] 愛慕；熱愛

Angie adores the man who saved her cat and believes him to be a hero.

affection
n. [U, sing.] af-fec-tion [ə`fɛkʃən] 喜愛

Even after being married for 40 years, the couple still showed a lot of affection to each other.

interact
vi. in-ter-act [ˏɪntɚ`ækt] 互動；交流

Scientists grouped children with different mother tongues together to see how they would interact with one another.

meaningful
adj. mean-ing-ful [`minɪŋfəl] 有意義的；重要的

All the employees had prepared a lot for the meeting, so they had a very meaningful discussion and came up with some great ideas.

fellow
adj. fel-low [`fɛlo] 同伴的；同事的

Don and his fellow workers usually spend their lunch break eating and chatting together.
